etudes
======

An etude is a common single-movement piano piece designed to develop the technical skill of the player. The literal translation from the French word *etude* is 'study'.

This package is my collection of self-styled or guided etudes in coding, mathematics, statistics, and data science.

My all-time favourite, never to be replaced, etude is Scriabin's *Etude in* C\# *minor* Op. 2 no. 1.

I first heard this piece of music as performed by the head of keyboard at the Con. In that moment I thought that my piano career would be complete if I could *play* that piece; that I would have finished some grand adventure.

I played it day, I played it night. I played it so much my neighbours banged on the walls of my shoebox apartment. I couldn't stop, I had to feel it under my fingers. A wonderfully visceral experience, throbbing with mid-twenties angst.

Pianistically, it was a good fit, despite the physical challenge of breadth. It relies heavily on *voicing technique*, one of the few pianistic techniques I genuinely improved at during my time at the Con.

Triumphantly, I recall ascending to the upper floor of the college of the arts to play for my teacher. The light was dappled through the broad green leaves that fell upon the paired concert grands in his office.

At *adagio*, the piece felt interminable and I was certain every note was not caressed lovingly enough. I reached the *fermata* on the final note and released it in fear, convinced by three the torturous unprecedented minutes of no interruption.

He verbally leapt upon me in a frenzy. That I would *fail* if I were to commit such an egregious error, to release a *fermata* before giving the note at the very least its full due.

My face fell.

"Oh, no," he said, "I'm only being so critical of that one note because the rest of it is so perfect! I have never heard you play so well."

I did feel complete with that, though, and am rather ashamed that I essentially lost interest in becoming a better pianist after that. Having started at seven-years old, it'd been twenty years, and it was time for something new.

Unfortunately, I was only half-way through my degree. The rest was a bit bumpy, sigh, as I became more and more certain this was not the career for me. Also, **no way** am I that tough.

I will always feel proud that I learnt to play this piece, by some measure, well. My favourite recording of this piece is, unsurprisingly, [Horowitz](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZnU0i52eG20).

Installation
------------

You can install etudes from github with:

``` r
# install.packages("devtools")
devtools::install_github("softloud/etudes")
```
